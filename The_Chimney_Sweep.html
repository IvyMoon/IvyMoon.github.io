The Chimney Sweep

The air was frigid, as was the chimney sweep's heart. Standing atop mansions, cleaning the nobility's chimneys was cumbersome work. Especially during the winter. His clothes were always soiled, smeared with black soot. His face, normally pale as snow, took on the ashen hue of someone who had already lost everything.

He guessed he had. Standing this particular morning, on the Grand Duke's chimney, he felt weary. The sun had not yet risen, and the air was cold. This was his favorite time of day. Nobody else was awake, giving him time to look up at the sky as dawn approached. He could see a faint glimmer of stars, fading as the sun slowly started to rise.

And then he thought of her. The only girl his heart would ever beat for. The one girl that he had always dreamed of being with. As far away from his reach as the moon. They had never spoken, except for an occasional hello when passing. She was the Grand Duke's daughter. As unapproachable as girls ever come. But when their eyes met, he knew that there was some deep connection between them. Like two ancient souls recognizing their shared past way back in time. He had never felt like this around any other girl, and it warmed his heart remembering their shared glances.

But alas, his heart was full of sorrow. Standing atop the chimney this frigid morning, covered in ash and soot, he started remembering what he wished he would not. Closing his eyes, he saw the girl before him. Her soulful blue eyes, long pale blond hair, and skin that smelled of moon lilies, intoxicating him with her very presence. He smiled sadly at the thought of her, and a tear slid slowly down his cheek.

Opening his eyes, more tears began to fall. Dawn was about to break. As he looked out over the horizon, he tried holding back quiet sobs. She was gone. And the memory of her imprisoned him in an endless pool of despair. A horse carriage accident had transported her from this life to the next. There would be no more tomorrows for her, and he was saddened by the thought of a future that could have been but never will be. Standing above everyone and everything on the chimney, he looked up at the sky and felt like he was one step closer to reaching her. Perhaps she was watching him from above. Suddenly, the sun peeked over the skyline, and a sliver of hope slid into his heart. Still consumed by grief, his heart was as still as the icy morning air. But the hint of sunshine granted him momentary reprieve from his gloom, reminding him of his dearly departed's radiance and love of all things light. And so, looking at the sunrise, he knew he would see her again someday. They were soulmates after all.

"My crazy pigeon flew away into the sunset.

At dawn, a dreamy dove flew into view.

At noon, a lucky duck crossed my path.

And as the sun set again, my crazy pigeon flew back to me."

"Somewhere over the rainbow...
pancakes fly...
Pancakes fly over the rainbow...
Why then, oh, why can't...
my......french......fry......

'Hot dog, hot dog, hot diggity dog,'
Said the pancake to the french fry,
As he beckoned him to fly
Over the rainbow.
But I can't leave without my dear ketchup!
Cried the french fry,
As he waddled back into the McDonald's box from which he came..."

"Where are my glasses, Bobbeth?
Shall I bobbeth for an apple?
Shall my apple bobbeth for its cousin, Adam Apple?
Shall Adam Apple bobbeth for Cousin Eve?
Does Adam Apple have an adam's apple?
Where art thou, Cousin Eve?
Is eve setting upon thou?
Is thy name before or after the setting sun?
The setting sun shalt never set nor rise.
For the Earth turneth on its own about the magnanimous sun.
Art thou in disagreement, my darling apple?
Art thou not a seed of the fruit we calleth life?
The Earth spinneth round and round, solitary in its revolution.
But feareth not, my lovely friend! For it spinneth among others.
As they maketh revolutions, Earth maketh revolutions.
And so, as thou hath learned in school, we all maketh revolutions,
As the bush hath gone round and round, thy shall go round and round.
And she will come round the mountain, feareth not my son, for the sun shall continue
All of our revolutions."

"The moon was luminescent, the sky was starry, and Humphrey was hungry.

Scampering across the luscious grass this fine evening, Humphrey let out a cry of agitation. Where was a garbage can?

Every night, Humphrey made his way to the trash, the epitome of what he considered fine dining. Half-eaten hamburgers, remnants of chocolate donuts, leftover noodles, and his all-time-favorite food, chicken.

This evening, Humphrey had arrived at his normal time (exactly 10:02 pm) at the residence of Mr. Ricardo.
But alas! Mr. Ricardo's trash can was missing.
Humphrey suspected his brother, Bob, had stolen it in retaliation for eating the delectable McDonald's hamburger they found earlier that week in Mr. Ricardo's trash. While Bob wasn't looking, Humphrey had stuffed the entire hamburger into his mouth with his paws.

When Bob asked what happened to the hamburger, Humphrey said it magically floated away into the second dimension of the universe. Bob raised his furry eyebrow at the proclamation but said nothing. He didn't buy it one bit.

And so presently, with no garbage can in sight, Humphrey sadly left Mr. Ricardo's yard in search of a meal elsewhere. Bob had made his point loud and clear. Share food with your raccoon brother, or else your garbage can will disappear."

"Do you see me?

No, silly frog. No, I do not.

Then, Mr. Banks, did you see the note I left you? It contains some very important information...

No, I have not. You are quite the bothersome frog, Willard. Shall I call security and have you thrown out the window like the pest you know you are?

No, sir. If I'm thrown out the window, I can't fly like you. You're a crane and I'm a frog. *hangs head in defeat, mumbles something completely incoherent, and walks out of Mr. Banks' office*

'Damn frog,' says the crane.

The End."

An evergreen leaf is attached to a tree.
It has been there since the spring,
Blowing in the wind,
And shivering in the cold night's air.
Winter is coming.
It knows that Winter is cold.
But more than that, it sees Winter's intent.
Winter doesn't just freeze things.
Winter consumes any warmth it can find,
Enveloping all the good in its icy embrace
And freezing away its hope.
So that all that's left is a deep pit of cold
Nothingness.
The evergreen leaf knows that Winter will consume it.
The evergreen leaf is awaiting its arrival with dread.
